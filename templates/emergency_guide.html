Emergency Guide (Voice + Images)

For life-threatening emergencies, call emergency services immediately (911 in the US, 112/108 in India).

1) Scene Safety and Alert

  1. Check if area is safe (traffic, fire, electricity, gas leak).
  2. Call emergency service immediately and share exact location.
  3. Ask one person to fetch first-aid kit and another to guide ambulance.

2) Check Response and Breathing

  1. Tap shoulder and ask loudly, "Are you okay?"
  2. If unresponsive and not breathing normally, start CPR immediately.

3) CPR (Adult, Bystander)

  1. Place person on firm surface, center of chest exposed.
  2. Hands in center of chest, elbows straight.
  3. Push hard and fast: 100-120 compressions per minute, about 5 cm deep.
  4. Allow full chest recoil after each compression.
  5. If trained, do 30 compressions + 2 rescue breaths; otherwise hands-only CPR continuously.

4) Severe Bleeding

  1. Wear gloves if available.
  2. Apply direct firm pressure with clean cloth or gauze.
  3. Add more layers if soaked; do not keep lifting the cloth.
  4. If limb bleeding is uncontrolled, apply tourniquet above wound if trained.

5) Choking (Conscious Adult)

  1. If person cannot speak or cough, act immediately.
  2. Give 5 back blows between shoulder blades.
  3. Give 5 abdominal thrusts.
  4. Repeat until object comes out or person becomes unresponsive.

6) Burns

  1. Remove from heat source safely.
  2. Cool burn under clean running water for 20 minutes.
  3. Remove tight items before swelling and cover with non-stick sterile dressing.
  4. Do not apply ice, toothpaste, ghee, oils, powder, or unknown substances.

7) Suspected Fracture or Head Injury

  1. Do not move person unless immediate danger exists.
  2. Immobilize injured part with splint support if trained.
  3. For head or neck trauma, keep neck straight and still.

8) Seizure

  1. Clear nearby hard or sharp objects.
  2. Turn person onto side after jerking slows.
  3. Do not force anything into mouth.
  4. Call ambulance if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes.

9) Heat Stroke

  1. Move person to cool shaded area.
  2. Remove excess clothing and cool with wet cloth, fan, or ice packs at neck, armpit, and groin.
  3. If unconscious or confused, do not give oral fluids; call ambulance urgently.
Ayurveda support can be used only after emergency stabilization and doctor review. Never delay first aid or ambulance activation.
